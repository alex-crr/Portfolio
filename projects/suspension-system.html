Project Description

This project tackled the challenge of designing an advanced suspension system for off-road vehicles that could maintain stability and comfort across varying terrain. Traditional suspension systems often compromise between on-road comfort and off-road capability, but our design aimed to excel at both.

Our approach incorporated adaptive damping technology with a multi-link architecture that allows for increased wheel articulation without sacrificing stability. We used computational modeling to optimize spring rates and damper characteristics across different loading conditions. The system includes a rapid-response hydraulic system that can adjust ride height based on terrain conditions detected by forward-facing sensors.
