Project Description

Our vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) design addresses the limitations of traditional horizontal axis turbines in urban environments. The helical blade configuration reduces noise and vibration while maintaining efficiency across a wide range of wind directions - crucial for the turbulent wind conditions found in cities.

Through computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations, we optimized the blade profile to achieve a 30% improvement in power coefficient compared to conventional Savonius designs. The turbine incorporates a novel magnetic bearing system that reduces mechanical losses and maintenance requirements. Our prototype generates 1.2kW at 12m/s wind speed with a cut-in speed of just 3m/s.
