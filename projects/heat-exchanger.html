Project Description

The Compact Heat Exchanger project focused on designing a highly efficient thermal transfer device for space-constrained applications. By utilizing advanced microchannel technology combined with innovative flow patterns, we achieved significant improvements in heat transfer coefficients while reducing the overall size and weight.

Our design incorporates fractal-inspired channel geometries that maximize surface area while minimizing pressure drop. We used computational fluid dynamics to optimize the flow distribution and identify potential areas of flow stagnation. The final design was manufactured using selective laser melting, allowing for complex internal geometries that would be impossible with traditional manufacturing methods.
